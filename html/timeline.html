Timeline

Year of Implosion:

2060

  1. 1800- 1870 Industrial Revolution Level of carbon dioxide gas (CO2) in the atmosphere, as later measured in ancient ice, is about 290 ppm (parts per million). Mean global temperature (1850-1890) is roughly 13.7°C. First Industrial Revolution. Coal, railroads, and land clearing speed up greenhouse gas emission, while better agriculture and sanitation speed up population growth.
  2. 1870-1910 Second Industrial Revolution Second Industrial Revolution. Fertilizers and other chemicals, electricity, and public health further accelerate growth.
  3. 1914-1918 WW1 World War I; governments learn to mobilize and control industrial societies.
  4. 1920-1925 Opening of Texas Gulf Opening of Texas and Persian Gulf oil fields inaugurates era of cheap energy.
  5. 1939-1945 WW2 World War II. Military grand strategy is largely driven by a struggle to control oil fields.
  6. 1963 Calculations suggest that feedback with water vapor could make the climate acutely sensitive to changes in CO2 level. First meeting of experts concerned with global warming warns that a rise in sea level is likely, with "immense flooding" of shorelines.
  7. 1965 Boulder, Colorado meeting on causes of climate change: Lorenz and others point out the chaotic nature of climate system and the possibility of sudden shifts.
  8. 1970 First Earth Day. Environmental movement attains strong influence, spreads concern about global degradation. Creation of US National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, the world's leading funder of climate research.Aerosols from human activity are shown to be increasing swiftly. Bryson claims they counteract global warming and may bring serious cooling.
  9. 1982 Greenland ice cores reveal drastic temperature oscillations in the space of a century in the distant past. Strong global warming since mid-1970s is reported, with 1981 the warmest year on record.
  10. 2008 Climate scientists (although not the public) recognize that even if all greenhouse gas emissions could be halted immediately, global warming will continue for millennia.
  11. 2015 Researchers find collapse of West Antarctic ice sheet is irreversible, will bring meters of sea-level rise over future centuries.Paris Agreement: nearly all nations pledge to set targets for their own greenhouse gas cuts and to report their progress. Solar electricity and wind power become economically competitive with fossil fuels in some regions.
  12. 2018 Damage from impacts — droughts, floods, tropical cyclones, wildfires, decline of wildlife—is seen sooner and at lower CO2 levels than expected. Mean global temperature is 14.7°C, the warmest in tens of thousands of years. Level of CO2 in the atmosphere is 405 ppm, the highest in millions of years.
  13. 2030 In summer average temperature will become unbearable. It will be too hot for humans and animals to survive.
  14. 2060 All the Glaciers will have melted,most islands will be under water, the earth will begin to overheat and within months become a fireball.
